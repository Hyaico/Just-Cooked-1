2. What are cookies and similar technologies?

  • Cookies are small text files stored on your browser or device when you visit a website.
  • Local storage and session storage are browser-based storage mechanisms similar to cookies.
  • SDKs, device identifiers and similar technologies are used within mobile apps to perform comparable functions (e.g. remembering you, measuring performance, maintaining security).

We refer to all of these together as “cookies” or “tracking technologies” in this Policy.

3. Where we use them

We may use cookies and similar technologies:

  • On our websites (including landing pages, marketing pages and legal pages).
  • Within the Just Cooked™ mobile app (iOS and Android), via:
    • Local storage and cache on your device
    • Device identifiers
    • App store / subscription identifiers
    • Third-party SDKs (e.g. authentication, crash reporting, paywall management)

4. Why we use cookies and tracking technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  1. Strictly necessary / essential
    • To operate the Service and enable core functionality.
    • Examples:
      • Keeping you logged in and managing your session.
      • Enforcing security and preventing abuse or fraudulent activity.
      • Remembering your basic settings and privacy choices.
  2. Functionality
    • To remember your choices and enhance your experience.
    • Examples:
      • Remembering your preferred language or region.
      • Remembering some app-level preferences so you don’t have to re-enter them.
  3. Performance and reliability
    • To understand how the Service performs and to detect issues (e.g. crashes).
    • Examples:
      • Crash logs and basic performance metrics.
      • Aggregate, non-identifying usage statistics (e.g. which screens are used most, whether a feature fails).
  4. Analytics and improvement
    • We use PostHog analytics to understand how users interact with the Service (e.g. how features are used, where users drop off, and what errors occur) so we can improve it.
    • Where required by applicable law (for example in the UK/EEA for non-essential analytics cookies), we will only enable PostHog tracking after you have provided consent via our cookie/consent mechanism.
  5. Marketing and advertising (not currently used)
    • We do not currently use advertising or marketing cookies on the Just Cooked™ website or in the app.
    • If this changes in future, we will:
      • Update this Policy, and
      • Implement appropriate consent mechanisms where required by law.

5. Types of cookies and technologies we use

5.1 Strictly necessary / essential

These are required for the Service to function. They may be set by us or by third parties acting on our behalf.

Examples include:

  • Session cookies to keep you signed in.
  • Security cookies to help detect and prevent malicious activity.
  • Authentication tokens used by our backend (e.g. Supabase) to verify your identity.
  • App store / subscription identifiers used to verify your Premium status.

You cannot disable these without breaking core functionality.

5.2 Functionality

These help remember settings and choices to make the Service more convenient.

Examples include:

  • Remembering that you are on a particular step or screen.
  • Remembering certain in-app UI or preference choices (where technically implemented).

If you disable these, the Service may still work but with reduced convenience.

5.3 Performance and reliability

These help us understand how the Service performs in aggregate and diagnose technical problems.

Examples include:

  • Crash reporting SDKs
  • Basic performance metrics, such as load times or error rates
  • Anonymous or aggregated statistics (e.g. which features are used most)

We use this information to maintain and improve the Service, not to build personal profiles for advertising.

5.4 Analytics

We use PostHog analytics. PostHog may use cookies, local storage or similar identifiers to recognise a returning browser/device and measure usage (for example, pages/screens viewed, button taps, timestamps and basic technical information like browser/device type). Where such analytics go beyond strictly necessary purposes, we will:

  • Configure them to minimise personal data where possible (e.g. IP truncation, aggregation).
  • Obtain any required consent (for example via a cookie banner on the website).

5.5 Advertising (not currently used)

We currently do not use advertising cookies or SDKs.

If we decide to introduce them in future, we will:

  • Update this Policy,
  • Provide clear information about what is used and by whom, and
  • Obtain consent where required before we set such cookies.

6. Cookies and tracking in the Just Cooked™ mobile app

Within the mobile app, we may use:

  • Local storage / on-device cache – to store your preferences or data needed for offline use.
  • Device identifiers – such as IDFV/IDFA (iOS) or Advertising ID (Android), subject to your device settings and any platform-level consent.
  • Authentication tokens and encrypted keys – to keep your account secure and maintain your session.
  • Subscription and entitlement identifiers – used by app stores and subscription management services to verify that you have access to Premium features.
  • SDKs for security, crash reporting and performance – to help detect issues and maintain reliability.

You can control some of these via your device settings (see Section 7).

7. Managing your cookie and tracking preferences

7.1 On the website (browser)

Most web browsers allow you to:

  • View which cookies are stored.
  • Delete cookies.
  • Block cookies for specific sites.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Clear local storage and similar data.

You can usually find these controls under your browser’s “Settings”, “Privacy” or “Security” menus.

Be aware:

  • Blocking or deleting cookies may cause some features of the website to work less reliably or not at all.
  • Strictly necessary cookies are required for basic operation.

7.2 In the mobile app

Control options include:

  • Operating system settings – for example:
    • Limiting ad tracking / resetting advertising IDs.
    • Controlling which apps can use mobile data, background refresh, etc.
  • App-level settings (where implemented) – you may be able to adjust certain privacy or telemetry settings within the Just Cooked™ app itself.

Disabling certain permissions or identifiers may reduce functionality or prevent some features from working correctly (e.g. login, subscription verification, push notifications).

8. Do Not Track and similar signals

Some browsers and devices allow you to send a “Do Not Track” (DNT) signal. There is currently no consistent industry standard for how to respond to these signals.

At present, we do not specifically respond to DNT signals. We will review this if and when an accepted standard emerges.

You can still use the other controls described in this Policy to manage cookies and tracking technologies.
